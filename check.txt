The stock market is a marketplace where investors buy and sell shares of publicly traded companies.
# Synopsis: The stock market allows people to trade ownership in companies.

It plays a crucial role in the economy by enabling companies to raise capital for growth and expansion, while providing individuals and institutions opportunities to invest and potentially earn returns.
# Synopsis: It helps companies get money and lets investors try to make profits.

Stock prices fluctuate based on supply and demand, company performance, and broader economic factors.
# Synopsis: Prices change due to market forces and company results.

Participating in the stock market involves risks, but it can also offer rewards for those who make informed investment decisions.
